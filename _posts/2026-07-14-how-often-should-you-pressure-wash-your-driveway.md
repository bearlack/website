If you've noticed your driveway looking a bit grey and patchy again not long after your last clean, you're not imagining it. The Gold Coast's warm, humid climate is exactly the kind of environment mould, algae and lichen love, so local driveways tend to need cleaning more often than they would further south.

## A general rule of thumb

For most Gold Coast homes, a full driveway pressure wash every **12 to 18 months** keeps concrete looking fresh without letting grime build up enough to stain permanently. That said, the right interval really depends on your property.

## What makes a driveway get dirty faster

- **Shade and moisture** &mdash; driveways under trees or that don't get much direct sun stay damp longer, which speeds up mould and algae growth
- **Nearby trees** &mdash; leaf litter, sap and bird droppings all feed organic growth
- **Coastal exposure** &mdash; salt air breaks down sealants and surfaces faster than inland areas
- **Sprinklers and irrigation** &mdash; regular overspray keeps sections of concrete consistently wet
- **Surface type** &mdash; exposed aggregate and unsealed concrete are more porous and trap grime more than sealed or coloured concrete

If your driveway ticks two or three of these boxes, it's worth checking every 6 to 12 months rather than waiting for the full 18.

## Signs it's time for a clean

- Dark or greenish patches, especially in shaded corners
- A slightly slippery feel underfoot when wet
- Grout lines or joints between pavers turning black
- Visible tyre marks or oil stains that hosing won't shift

## Why staying on top of it matters

Beyond the way it looks, mould and algae left to build up can make concrete and pavers slippery, and organic growth left untreated tends to come back faster and thicker each time. Regular cleaning, paired with an eco-safe post-treatment, keeps growth from taking hold in the first place, so each clean stays easier than the last.
