Gutters are one of those things nobody thinks about until they overflow, sag, or start leaking into the ceiling. By then, the damage is usually already done. A bit of regular attention goes a long way.

## What happens when gutters are neglected

Leaves, twigs and sediment build up over time, especially if you've got overhanging trees nearby. Once a gutter is blocked, rainwater has nowhere to go &mdash; it spills over the edge instead of running through the downpipes, which is exactly what gutters are there to prevent.

## The benefits of staying on top of it

- **Prevents water damage.** Overflowing gutters send water straight down your fascia, walls and sometimes foundations. Over time that can mean rot, cracked render, or water finding its way inside.
- **Protects your roofline and fascia boards.** Constant overflow keeps the timber or fascia behind your gutters damp, which speeds up rot and can be a costly repair.
- **Reduces pest problems.** Stagnant water sitting in blocked gutters is an easy breeding ground for mosquitoes, and the leaf litter itself can attract birds and rodents looking for a place to nest.
- **Extends the life of the gutters themselves.** Wet leaf debris sitting against metal gutters speeds up corrosion. Keeping them clear means they last longer before needing replacement.
- **Lowers fire risk.** Dry leaves and debris sitting in gutters are exactly the kind of fuel that catches embers during bushfire season. Clear gutters are a simple, practical safety measure.
- **Saves you money in the long run.** A gutter clean is a small, routine cost. Fascia repairs, roof leaks and water damage to walls or foundations are not.

## How often is enough?

For most Gold Coast homes, once or twice a year is a reasonable baseline &mdash; more often if you've got overhanging trees, or after a run of storms, which tend to bring down a lot of debris at once.
