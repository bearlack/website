We're based right here in Southport, so it's the suburb we know best &mdash; and it throws up a few specific challenges when it comes to keeping a home looking its best.

## Life near the Broadwater comes with a trade-off

Southport's biggest drawcard is also part of the problem: living close to the Broadwater means salt-laden air moving through the suburb constantly, whether you're in a highrise near the water or a character home a few streets back. That salt air speeds up grime build-up on windows, accelerates staining on roofs, and gives mould and mildew a head start on house exteriors compared to suburbs further inland.

## A suburb of two very different housing styles

Southport has one of the more varied housing mixes on the Gold Coast &mdash; waterfront apartments and townhouses on one side, and established houses and older Queenslanders (especially around the TSS precinct) on the other. Both need different approaches:

- **Apartments and townhouses** tend to have more window area exposed to salt spray and need regular window cleaning to stay looking sharp, especially anything facing the water.
- **Older established homes** often have roofs and exterior cladding that have had years to accumulate moss, algae and grime, and benefit from a proper soft wash rather than a quick surface rinse.

## What we'd recommend for a typical Southport property

- **Roof washing** every 1 to 2 years rather than the 2 to 3 year guide we'd give further inland, given the exposure near the water.
- **Window cleaning** on a quarterly plan if you're close to the Broadwater, since salt residue and hard water spotting build up faster here than most other parts of the Gold Coast.
- **House washing** at the first sign of green or black staining, rather than waiting &mdash; the coastal humidity means it spreads faster once it takes hold.
